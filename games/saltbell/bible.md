# The Salt-Iron Bell — story bible

PREMISE. A courier carries a sealed letter up the Salt-Iron Pass to the Mirror-Bell
Sect, an order grown old and small. The letter has no name on its face. By the time
the courier reaches the hall, it is clear the letter decides whether the sect's last
master lives out the winter or is called down to answer for an old debt.

VOICE. Second person, present tense for action, past for memory. Plain, weighted,
a little cold. One sensory anchor per scene (salt, iron, smoke, cold stone). No
ornament for its own sake.

CHARACTERS.
- The courier (you): road-worn, paid to carry, not to care. Tested anyway.
- Ren: the gatekeeper. Lean, dry-humoured, keeps a riddle at the gate because he is
  bored and because it sorts the worthy from the merely punctual. Introduced at the gate.
- Master Lou: the sect's last master, blind, calm, ready. Introduced in the hall.

PLACES. The Salt-Iron Pass — a cold notch in the mountains where the rock tastes of
salt and the old mines bled iron into the stream.

LORE. The salt-iron bell hangs in the hall; rung once for a death, twice for a debt.
Its voice carries the length of the valley.

MOTIFS. Salt, iron, weight, the sealed thing, the choice to know or not to know.
